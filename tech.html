Why STEM?

We need STEM. In the 21st century, scientific innovations have become increasingly important as we face the benefits and challenges of a knowledge-based economy. To succeed in this world, students need to develop their capabilities in STEM. You're taking the first step by visiting this site.

Girls Who Code

A great 7 week program that teaches girls about computer science, leadership, robots, and teamwork. Bonus: Cool Field Trips

Code.org

Backed by some of the biggest names in tech, it's mission is to grow computer science learning by fostering its inclusion in school curriculums.

TechRocket

TechRocket's courses for coding, game design, and graphic design are built on the premise that "you're never too young to learn STEM skills."

Gamestar Mechanic

Learn to design your own video game! Explore game-based quests and take courses to build your skills. Feel free to try it out!

Machinarium

An incredibly slick point-and-click adventure game. You’re a robot who’s been tossed on a scrap heap and must solve a series of puzzles.

Robots for iPad App

Everything you want to know about robots in one easy app. Robots for iPad has 360-degree views, lots of articles and specs and hundreds of videos.

Ambition Program

Boldly go where no kid has gone before. Immerse yourself in a six-day aviation-themed learning adventure at the National Flight Academy in Florida.

Digital Media Summer Camp for Teens

A camp about game design and programming, filmmaking and visual effects or 3-D and animation.

iD Tech Camps

Make your own video game. Program your own app. Code in Java. At iD Tech’s day and overnight camps, practically anything is possible.
